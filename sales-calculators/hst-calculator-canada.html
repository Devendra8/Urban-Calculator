Different types of sales taxes in Canada?

The three main sales taxes here in Canada are GST, HST, and PST although we have RST which is for Manitoba, and QSC which is for Quebec but here we'll focus on the first three ones.

GST stands for goods and services taxes and it's Canada's federal sales tax based on the province that your business is registered. It can show up in two different ways, it can be a separate five percent tax or it can be HST or harmonize sales tax which is a combination of GST and PSD. If you're running your businesses in Ontario, Nova Scotia, and these provinces over here, you have to use harmonized sales tax which is GST plus PSD which is somewhere between 13 to 15 percent.

On the other hand, PSD is a province-based sales tax that you collect separately from the GST. For example, here where I live in British Columbia or Saskatchewan, We collect pst which is seven percent, and GST which is five percent separately as you can see on this chart. Over here GST sales tax rates are different from the province. For example, in Alberta, you should collect only five percent GST, in Ontario, you should collect 13 percent HST, in Quebec, it's a 9.97 QSD and 5 percent GST separately.
