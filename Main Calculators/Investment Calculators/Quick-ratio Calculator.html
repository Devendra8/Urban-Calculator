Quick Ratio Calculator

What is Quick-ratio?

The quick ratio is an indicator of a company’s short-term liquidity position and measures a company’s ability to satisfy its short-term obligations with its most liquid assets .

Quick-ratio formula

The formula to calculate the quick ratio is:

Quick-ratio = (MS + AR + CE)/CL
Where,
    MS = Market Securities
    AR = Accounts Receivable
    CE = Cash equivalents
    CL = Current Liabilities

How do you calculate quick ratio?

Given values is the balance sheet of company abc:


Cash  $1000
Market Securities  $8000
Accounts Receivable  $5000
Current Liabilities  $7000


    Then the value of quick ratio is :
     QR= (1000+8000+5000)/7000 = 2

Usage of Quick-ratio

• When a company has a quick ratio of 1, its quick assets are equal to its current assets. This also indicates that the company can pay off its current debts without selling its long-term assets.

• If a company has a quick ratio higher than 1, this means that it owns more quick assets than current liabilities.

What is a good quick ratio percentage?

The quick ratio represents the amount of short-term marketable assets available to cover short-term liabilities, and a good quick ratio is 1 or higher. The greater this number, the more liquid assets a company has to cover its short-term obligations and debts.

What is a bad quick ratio?

If your business has a quick ratio of 1.0 or greater, that typically means your business is healthy and can pay its liabilities. The greater the number, the better off your business is. A high quick ratio means your business is financially secure in the short-term future.
