What is Enterprise Value?

Enterprise Value (EV) is that the measure of a company’s total value. it's at the whole market price instead of just the equity value, so all ownership interests and asset claims from both debt and equity are included. EV are often thought of because the effective cost of shopping for a corporation or the theoretical price of a takeover target.

Enterprise Value formula

The extended formula is:

EV = Common Shares + Preferred Shares + Market Value of Debt + Minority Interest – Cash and Equivalents

Usage of Enterprise Value

• Enterprise Value is often used for multiples such as EV/EBITDA, EV/EBIT, EV/FCF, or EV/Sales for comparable analysis such as trading comps.

• Enterprise Value is very useful in Mergers and Acquisition situations, especially with controlling ownership interests.
