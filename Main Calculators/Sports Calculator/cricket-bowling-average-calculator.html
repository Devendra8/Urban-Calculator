What is Bowling Average?

In cricket, a player's bowling average is the number of runs they have conceded per wicket taken. The lower the bowling average is, the better the bowler is performing.

What is a good Bowling average?

Test bowling averages over a career are mostly between 20 and 50. Averages are roughly considered as follows: Under 20: An exceptionally good bowler. 20 to 25: A very good bowler.

How do you calculate an Cricket Bowling Average?

Bowling Average = Runs Conceded / Wickets Taken

Example of Bowling Average?

If a bowler has conceded 72 runs has taken 7 wickets in that time, then:
72 divided by 7 is 10.2857
Therefore, the bowler’s bowling average is 10.2857.
