What is WHIP?

It is a measure of a pitcher’s ability to prevent batters from reaching base. Comparing to ERA, WHIP measures a pitcher’s effectiveness against the batters faced more directly.

What is a good WHIP stat in baseball??

A good WHIP for a pitcher is around 1.00. Anything below 1.00 is outstanding and demonstrates the domination of a pitcher. A poor WHIP is anything over 1.75. That means for each innings pitched there is a good chance two or more runners will reach base.

How do you calculate an WHIP?

WHIP = (Number of hits allowed + Number of walks allowed) / Number of Innings Pitched

Do you want a low WHIP?

WHIP reflects a pitcher's propensity for allowing batters to reach base, therefore a lower WHIP indicates better performance.
